About the Program

Interactive media design is a two year program that specializes in arts and design including motion graphics and coding. It gives students the practical hands on skills they need to code, use video and work with graphic design. First year is broad, introducing students to the many disciplines this program has to offer. It is more so focused on the planning and development process, making students familiar with the creating process. Second year on the other hand is more specific and introduces students to more of a real world, course based atmosphere. Individualistic and teachers courses take place, giving students an idea of what to expect in the real world, while carrying out courses start to finish. A Co-Op term is also offered to high achieving students. Second year also gives the students choices as to whether they want to specialize more so in coding or video. A mandatory motion graphics course is also included, which applies to those who have a passion for graphic design.

If you like the following things, then you will most likely enjoy your time in this program:

Web Design

Thanks to the exponentional growth of web activity, students must learn to design for a platform that keeps evolving each and every day

Photography/Video

Learning how to work in these fields of multimedia is crucial nowadays. Students will learn how to take professional shots, as well as tweak and edit them later on

Teamwork

What is the use of coming up with a great idea if you won't share it. Building bonds with your fellow students and working alongside them will prepare you for the outside world

Web Production

MTM6330

Web Production is a 3rd semester web design course. In it, you will learn how to use:

  • GitHub to host your websites via repositories
  • SASS, which is a CSS extension with endless possibilities
  • Bootstrap, being a about that gives you a variety of tools to make coding much more efficient

This course builds up on the previous web design courses from past semesters. The student must already have a good knowledge of HTML, CSS, and Javascript for the best learning experience.

This class is taught by Robert McKenney, who has years of experience in web design, especially SASS. He is a great teacher to have if you are serious about getting into the amazing world of web design.

There are a total of 10 in-class exercises, 2 assignments, and a handfull of quizzes

Video Foundations

MTM6200

Video Foundation is a 2rd semester videography and post-production course. In it, you will learn:

  • Videography techniques, and how to use the proper equipment in order to get the best quality video
  • How to edit your video and audio using Adobe Premiere and Adobe Audition, respectively
  • Where to find the best shooting locations, and how to use your environment to enhance your video's quality

This course will show you how much the film industry is changing every day, and it is adapted to the current filming standards.

This class is taught by Christopher Paine and Kaan Bayulken. They both have been working in the video and photography fields for a long time, and they are more than happy to share their knowledge with all that want to learn.

There are a total of 6 assignments, and in-class practical exercises every week

Motion Graphics

MTM6300

Motion Graphics, like the name suggests, is a 3rd semester motion graphics course. In it, you will learn:

  • How to use Adobe After Effects in order to make add effects to your videos
  • The right way to set up your work environment and how to organize your files
  • Make videos by only using assets made within Adobe After Effects.

This course goes great alongside the Video Foundations course, because motion graphics and videography can be used together to make a truly beautiful work of art.

This class is taught by Hussein Tayrani. He has a lot of experience in the video and motion graphics fields. He also knows a lot about content management systems, which is a class he taught in the 2nd semester.

There are a total of 3 assignments, a couple of quizzes, and an in-class exercise each week.
